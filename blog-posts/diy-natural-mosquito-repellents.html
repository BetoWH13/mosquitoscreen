DIY Natural Mosquito Repellents That Actually Work

Published on February 5, 2025

Looking for chemical-free ways to keep mosquitoes at bay? These DIY natural mosquito repellents are simple to make, cost-effective, and surprisingly effective. Here are the best options you can try today.

Why It Works: Lemon eucalyptus oil contains citronellal, a proven natural mosquito repellent.

How to Make:

  • Mix 10 drops of lemon eucalyptus oil with 2 tablespoons of witch hazel or carrier oil (like coconut or olive oil).
  • Add 1/4 cup of water and shake well.
  • Spray on exposed skin (avoid eyes and mouth).

üçã 2. DIY Citronella Candles

Why It Works: Citronella masks scents that attract mosquitoes.

How to Make:

  • Melt soy wax and add 20-30 drops of citronella essential oil.
  • Pour into a heat-safe container with a wick.
  • Light the candle outdoors to keep bugs away.

üå± 3. Herbal Mosquito Repellent Sachets

Why It Works: Dried herbs like lavender, rosemary, and mint naturally repel mosquitoes.

How to Make:

  • Fill small cloth bags with dried lavender, mint, and rosemary.
  • Place sachets near windows, doors, or in outdoor seating areas.

üçä 4. Clove and Lime Natural Repellent

Why It Works: The combination of cloves and citrus deters mosquitoes effectively.

How to Make:

  • Cut a lime in half and insert whole cloves into the flesh.
  • Place the lime halves around your outdoor space.

üåº 5. Lavender Oil Body Spray

Why It Works: Lavender has a calming scent for humans but is disliked by mosquitoes.

How to Make:

  • Mix 10 drops of lavender essential oil with 2 tablespoons of distilled water and 1 tablespoon of witch hazel.
  • Pour into a spray bottle and apply to the skin.
